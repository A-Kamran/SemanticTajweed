# Comprehensive List of Tajweed Rules

## 1. Alif and Laam Rules

| Initial Name               | Arabic Name (Arabic)         | Description                                                             | Simplified Name                |
|----------------------------|------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------|
|Alif Wasl|	أَلِف وَصْل|	Silent Alif at the beginning of words, pronounced when continuing but silent when stopping.|	Silent Alif|
|Laam Al-Qamari|	لَام قَمَرِيَّة|	Clear pronunciation of Laam before Qamari letters (e.g., القمر).|	Clear Laam|
|Laam Ash-Shamsi|	لَام شَمْسِيَّة|	Silent Laam before Shamsi letters, with Shadda on the next letter (e.g., الشمس).|	Silent Laam|
|Laam Jalalah|	لَام جَلَالَة|	Heavy (Tafkheem) or light (Tarqeeq) pronunciation of Laam in "Allah" depending on the preceding vowel.|	Laam of Allah|

## 2. Ghunnah and Idghaam Rules

| Initial Name               | Arabic Name (Arabic)         | Description                                                             | Simplified Name                |
|----------------------------|------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------|
|Ghunnah-Complete|	غُنَّة كَامِلَة|	Full nasal sound with Noon or Meem accompanied by Shadda, pronounced clearly and fully.|	Complete Ghunnah|
|Idghaam-Mutajanisaan|	إِدْغَام مُتَجَانِسَيْنِ|	Assimilation of letters with similar articulation points but different characteristics.|	Similar Letters Assimilation|
|Idghaam-Mutaqaribaan|	إِدْغَام مُتَقَارِبَيْنِ|	Assimilation of letters with closely related articulation points.|	Close Letters Assimilation|
|Idghaam Ash-Shafawi|	إِدْغَام شَفَوِي|	Merging of Meem Sakinah with Ba, producing a nasal sound.|	Shafawi Idghaam|
|Idghaam With Ghunnah|	إِدْغَام بِغُنَّة|	Merging of Noon Sakinah or Tanween with (ي ن م و) with nasalization.|	Idghaam with Ghunnah|
|Idghaam With Ghunnah-Complete|	إِدْغَام بِغُنَّة كَامِلَة|	Full nasal assimilation with Shadda, ensuring a complete nasal sound.|	Complete Idghaam with Ghunnah|
|Idghaam With Ghunnah-InComplete|	إِدْغَام بِغُنَّة غَيْر كَامِلَة|	Partial nasal assimilation without Shadda, producing a lighter nasal sound.|	Incomplete Idghaam with Ghunnah|
|Idghaam Without Ghunnah|	إِدْغَام بِغَيْر غُنَّة|	Merging without nasalization when Noon Sakinah or Tanween is followed by ر or ل.|	Idghaam without Ghunnah|

## 3. Ikhfa and Iqlab Rules

| Initial Name               | Arabic Name (Arabic)         | Description                                                             | Simplified Name                |
|----------------------------|------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------|
|Ikhfa|	إِخْفَاء|	Concealment of Noon Sakinah or Tanween, producing a nasal sound between Idhaar and Idghaam.|	Concealment (Ikhfa)|
|Ikhfa Ash-Shafawi|	إِخْفَاء شَفَوِي|	Concealment of Meem Sakinah when followed by Ba, producing a light nasal sound.|	Shafawi Concealment|
|Iqlab|	إِقْلَاب|	Conversion of Noon Sakinah or Tanween into Meem before Ba, with complete nasalization.|	Conversion (Iqlab)|

## 4. Qalqalah Rules

| Initial Name               | Arabic Name (Arabic)         | Description                                                             | Simplified Name                |
|----------------------------|------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------|
|Qalqalah|	قَلْقَلَة|	Echoing sound produced with Sukoon on Qalqalah letters (ق ط ب ج د), creating a bouncing effect.|	Echoing Sound|
|Lesser Qalqalah|	قَلْقَلَة صُغْرَى|	Weaker echoing sound compared to Greater Qalqalah, usually in the middle of a word.|	Weak Echoing Sound|
|Greater Qalqalah|	قَلْقَلَة كُبْرَى|	Strong echoing sound on Qalqalah letters at the end of a word or with a stopping pause.|	Strong Echoing Sound|


## 6. Tafkheem and Tarqeeq (Heaviness and Lightness) Rules

| Initial Name               | Arabic Name (Arabic)         | Description                                                             | Simplified Name                |
|----------------------------|------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------|
|Tafkheem-Alif|	تَفْخِيم أَلِف|	Heaviness in Alif pronunciation when preceded by certain letters or sounds.|	Heavy Alif|
|Tafkheem-Highest|	تَفْخِيم أَقْوَى|	Strongest form of heaviness, usually with heavy letters in prominent positions.|	Strongest Heaviness|
|Tafkheem-Laam Jalalah|	تَفْخِيم لَام جَلَالَة|	Heaviness in Laam of "Allah" after Fatha or Damma.|	Heavy Laam of Allah|
|Tafkheem-Lowest|	تَفْخِيم أَضْعَف|	Weakest form of heaviness in certain phonetic contexts.|	Weakest Heaviness|
|Tafkheem-Middle|	تَفْخِيم مُتَوَسِّط|	Medium level of heaviness depending on the surrounding letters.|	Medium Heaviness|
|Tafkheem-Raa|	تَفْخِيم رَاء|	Heavy pronunciation of Raa depending on the preceding and following vowels.|	Heavy Raa|
|Tarqeeq-Alif|	تَرْقِيق أَلِف|	Light pronunciation of Alif in certain contexts.|	Light Alif|
|Tarqeeq-Laam|	تَرْقِيق لَام|	Light pronunciation of Laam depending on context.|	Light Laam|
|Tarqeeq-Raa|	تَرْقِيق رَاء|	Light pronunciation of Raa when preceded or followed by a Kasra sound.|	Light Raa|

## Medd (Elongation) Rules

| Initial Name               | Arabic Name (Arabic)         | Description                                                             | Simplified Name                |
|----------------------------|------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------|
|Medd-Alif|	مَدْ أَلِف|	Elongation of Alif vowel sound by two Harakaat when preceded by Fatha.|	Alif Elongation|
|Medd-Dagger Alif|	مَدْ أَلِف خَنْجَرِيَّة|	Short elongation with a small Alif symbol above certain letters, pronounced for two Harakaat.|	Short Alif Elongation|
|Medd-Wow|	مَدْ وَاو|	Elongation of Wow vowel sound by two Harakaat when preceded by Damma.|	Wow Elongation|
|Medd-Wow-SmallWow|	مَدْ وَاو صَغِير|	Short elongation with a small Wow symbol.|	Small Wow Elongation|
|Medd-Yaa|	مَدْ يَاء|	Elongation of Yaa vowel sound by two Harakaat when preceded by Kasra.|	Yaa Elongation|
|Medd-Yaa-SmallHighYaa|	مَدْ يَاء صَغِير عَلَىٰ|	Short elongation of Yaa with a small high Yaa symbol.|	Small High Yaa Elongation|
|Medd-Yaa-SmallYaa|	مَدْ يَاء صَغِير|	Short elongation of Yaa with a small Yaa symbol.|	Small Yaa Elongation|
|Medd Al-Arid Sukoon|	مَدْ عَارِض السُّكُون|	Temporary elongation when stopping on a word ending with Sukoon, ranging from 2 to 6 Harakaat.|	Temporary Elongation|
|Medd Al-Ewad|	مَدْ عِوَض|	Elongation of Tanween Fatha at the end of a verse when stopping, replacing it with Alif sound.|	Substitute Elongation|
|Medd As-Sila|	مَدْ صِلَة|	Elongation of the pronoun Ha when followed by Hamza, pronounced as a vowel extension.|	Connecting Elongation|
|Medd As-Sila-SmallWow|	مَدْ صِلَة وَاو صَغِير|	Short elongation with a small Wow symbol for the pronoun Ha.|	Small Wow Connection|
|Medd As-Sila-SmallYaa|	مَدْ صِلَة يَاء صَغِير|	Short elongation with a small Yaa symbol for the pronoun Ha.|	Small Yaa Connection|
|Medd As-Sila Kubra|	مَدْ صِلَة كُبْرَى|	Extended elongation of the pronoun Ha when followed by Hamza, pronounced up to 6 Harakaat.|	Extended Connecting Elongation|
|Medd As-Sila Wow Kubra|	مَدْ صِلَة وَاو كُبْرَى|	Extended elongation with a small Wow symbol, pronounced up to 6 Harakaat.|	Extended Wow Connection|
|Medd As-Sila Yaa Kubra|	مَدْ صِلَة يَاء كُبْرَى|	Extended elongation with a small Yaa symbol, pronounced up to 6 Harakaat.|	Extended Yaa Connection|
|Medd Laazim Kalmi M|	مَدْ لَازِم كَلْمِي مُخَفَّف|	Mandatory elongation due to Sukoon in the middle of a word, pronounced for 6 Harakaat.|	Mandatory Word Elongation (Middle)|
|Medd Laazim Kalmi MK|	مَدْ لَازِم كَلْمِي مُثَقَّل|	Mandatory elongation with Sukoon at the end of a word, pronounced for 6 Harakaat.|	Mandatory Word Elongation (End)|
|Medd Lazim|	مَدْ لَازِم|	Necessary elongation due to Sukoon or Shadda, pronounced for 6 Harakaat.|	Necessary Elongation|
|Medd Munfasil-Derived|	مَدْ مُنْفَصِل مُشْتَق|	Separated elongation between two words, when one ends with a vowel and the next begins with Hamza.|	Separated Elongation|
|Medd Munfasil-Primary|	مَدْ مُنْفَصِل أَصْلِي|	Primary form of separated elongation, pronounced for 2 to 5 Harakaat.|	Primary Separated Elongation|
|Medd Muttasil-Derived|	مَدْ مُتَّصِل مُشْتَق|	Joined elongation within a single word when a vowel is followed by Hamza.|	Joined Elongation|
|Medd Muttasil-Primary|	مَدْ مُتَّصِل أَصْلِي|	Primary form of joined elongation, pronounced for 4 to 6 Harakaat.|	Primary Joined Elongation|
|Medd Wow Leen|	مَدْ وَاو لِين|	Soft elongation of Wow when preceded by Fatha and followed by Sukoon.|	Soft Wow Elongation|
|Medd Yaa Leen|	مَدْ يَاء لِين|	Soft elongation of Yaa when preceded by Fatha and followed by Sukoon.|
|Original Medd|	مَدْ أَصْلِي|	General elongation rule for Alif, Wow, and Yaa when they are preceded by Fatha, Damma, and Kasra respectively.|	Original Elongation|
